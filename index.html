The EquiBirth study

Improving information, choice and respect for individuals from Black and Brown communities during pregnancy

Take part

Why is the project needed?

Studies into maternity care have shown that individuals from Black and Brown communities are not always treated with respect and do not always get enough information about their health choices.

What is the aim of the project?

The EquiBirth study will work with individuals who are accessing/ have accessed maternity care, representatives from organisations working with individuals accessing care, and healthcare professionals to come up with ways to make things better.

What does the project involve?

The Equibirth project has three parts.

  1. Part 1 (realist review) involves looking at what has already been tried or learned about the topic.
  2. Part 2 involves working with women/birthing people and others to come up with ways to make things better.
  3. Part 3 is about checking that these ideas make things better, and how and where they work best.
