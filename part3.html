Part 3: A Delphi study

Reaching national consensus on how to measure avoidable health differences and avoidable differences in care during pregnancy: A Delphi study

Why is the project needed?

At present there is no national agreement on how to measure avoidable health differences in pregnancy.

The aim of this study is to bring:

  • those who experience avoidable health differences,
  • health care professionals who work with pregnant women, and
  • researchers

    • together to agree what we need to measure and how it should be measured. This is important if we are to understand why and how these avoidable health differences occur. This is called a Delphi study.

How will this project help?

If we use the same measures across the UK we can:

  • identify areas of good practice we can learn from,
  • identify areas in need of improvement,
  • compare different changes to understand which changes work best,
  • Understand who a change benefits and in what settings it works best, and
  • compare or combine the results of different research studies.

What does taking part involve?

The study consists of 2 survey's.

Prior to completing survey 1 or 2 you will be asked to watch a short video.

Survey 1 will list things we could measure. You will be asked which measures you think are important, and asked to provide the reasons behind your decision.

As part of survey 2 you will be shown the results of survey 1. Survey 2 will then ask you about the measures you think are important and why.

A measure will make the final list if over 70% of those taking part in survey 2 agree that the measure would inform work on avoidable health differences
