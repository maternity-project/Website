What does the project involve?

Part one of the project involves a Critical Interpretative Synthesis of the literature on avoidable health differences.

It asks us to question how well we understand avoidable health differences. It asks us to question how we have described and studied the problem, and how this has affected the solutions proposed. It helps us think about the problem in new ways, which helps us find new solutions.

What is the aim of the project?

The aim is to develop a model (a picture) that explains why and how avoidable health differences arise.

How will developing this model help us prevent and reduce avoidable health differences?

Preventing avoidable health differences requires us to understand why and how they have arisen in the first place, and why they continues to occur. Once we know the answer to these questions we can develop and test changes that will hopefully stop these avoidable differences arising in the first place and /or reduce the harm they cause.

How can I help?

If you have used maternity services in the UK in the last 5 years we want to know your views on the topics covered in the review, and hear your experiences.

If you work within maternity in the UK we would also like you to contribute to the review by sharing your views and experiences.

Your involvement will ensure that the model reflects your day to day experiences.
