What's Involved

How can I help?

By taking part, you will help make sure the changes are useful, easy to do, and that they work

Do I have to take part in the study?

No, it is up to you. You will have the chance to talk with someone from the research team. They will explain what taking part involves and will answer any questions you have. You can leave the study at any time, and you don't have to explain why. If you decide to leave your care will not be affected. Any information already collected will be kept and used, but no new information will be collected.

What does taking part involve?

The EquiBirth study has different parts. You can choose to take part in just one part, or more than one. The different parts include:

  • Focus groups
  • Initial workshop
  • Final workshop
  • Interviews
  • Observations of your care
  • Online survey

Focus groups

A focus group is when a small group of people come together to talk about their ideas on a certain topic. In this case, you will be asked to share your thoughts on different change ideas and help pick the best ones for the study.

There will be two focus groups.

One focus group will be for women/birthing people. It will take place in a community location in Nottingham. The focus group will last up to 90 minutes. We will provide snacks and drinks, pay for reasonable travel costs, and can provide an interpreter if needed.

The second focus group will be for professionals and people who work with women or people giving birth. This group will meet online and last up to 90 minutes.

The research team will take notes during the meetings. These notes will be anonymous, which means they won't include your name or any details that could identify you.

Initial workshop

At the workshop a small group of people will work together to answer a few important questions. Your answers will help us choose the best change ideas and decide the best way to test if they work.

There will be two workshops. One for women/birthing people and one for professionals and patient representatives.

For women/birthing people:

  • This workshop will happen in a community space in Nottingham
  • It will last up to 90 minutes
  • You can get money back for travel costs
  • Interpreters can be provided if needed

For professionals and patient representatives:

  • This workshop will happen online
  • It will last up to 90 minutes

The research team will take notes during the meetings. These notes will be anonymous, which means they won't include your name or any details that could identify you.

Final workshop

At the workshop a small group of people will work together to choose which change ideas should move to the next part of the study.

We are inviting women/birthing people, professionals, and patient representatives to join the final co-design workshop. This workshop will last up to two and a half hours and will take place in a community space in Nottingham. Travel costs will be covered, and interpreters can be provided if needed.

The research team will take notes during the meetings. These notes will be anonymous, which means they won't include your name or any details that could identify you.

Interviews

A researcher will ask what you think about the change ideas being tested. We are looking for women/birthing people and professionals to take part.

  • The interview can be in person at a place you choose or via a video call
  • It will take no more than one hour
  • You can get money back for travel costs
  • Interpreters can be provided if needed

You will be asked if it's okay to record the interview. This helps the research team write down what is said later. The written notes will not include your name or any other details that could show who you are

Observing the care you receive

If you are a woman/birthing person receiving maternity care from Nottingham University Hospitals Trust:

  • You may be asked if a member of the research team can watch part of your care
  • If you agree, a member of the research team would sit in during your appointment with a midwife or doctor
  • This helps the team see what care looks like before and during the testing of new ideas

If you are a professional providing maternity care at Nottingham University Hospitals Trust:

  • You may be asked if a member of the research team can watch part of the care you give
  • This is to understand what care looks like before and during the testing of new ideas

Any notes the team takes will not include names or details that could show who you are.

Online survey

The survey will ask you a few questions about the change ideas being tested. It will take up to 15 minutes to finish.

What are the possible benefits of taking part?

Right now, we don't know the best way to improve respect, information, and choice. The EquiBirth study wants to help find the answers. Making improvements takes time. This means those taking part might not see the benefits themselves, but we hope the study will help improve care for women and families in the future.

What are the possible disadvantages of taking part?

Taking part will take up some of your time.

Sensitive topics might come up in discussions. If that happens, the study team can help you find support. You can contact the study team anytime during the study by emailing Naomi.Taylor5@nhs.net.

Focus groups and workshops will be held in community venues in Nottingham so some travel may be required. We can pay for reasonable travel costs.

What will happen to my data?

We will keep all the information about you safe and secure

We need to use some information about you for this study. This might include your name and contact details. If you agree to let us watch your care, we will also ask to look at your medical records. This helps us see how information given to you and talks with you are recorded.

Only the people who need to know your name and contact details will see them. For everyone else, your information will have a code instead of your name.

Your information will be kept safe for 5 years. After that, it will be deleted.

What happens if I don't want to carry on with the study?

If you decide you don't want to continue with the study, no more information will be collected from you or your medical records. Any information already collected will be kept safe and used in the analysis.

Your care will not be affected if you choose to leave the study.

What happens when the study is finished?

When the study is finished, the results will be shared on this website. You can choose to get a short summary sent to you by email after the study ends.

What if there is a problem or I have a question?

You are welcome to contact the research team at any time. Please email Naomi.Taylor5@nhs.net and we will get back to you as soon as possible
